Poker Rules

Rule Description
Card Values Cards are ranked in ascending order: 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, Jack (J), Queen (Q), King (K), Ace (A).
The Ace can be worth either 1 or 14, so the possible straights are:
  • Lowest Straight: Ace (1), 2, 3, 4, 5
  • Highest Straight: 10, Jack (J), Queen (Q), King (K), Ace (14)
The four suits are hearts (♥), diamonds (♦), clubs (♣), and spades (♠).

Hand Rankings
  • High Card: The highest card in a hand.
  • Pair: Two cards of the same value.
  • Two Pair: Two sets of pairs.
  • Three of a Kind: Three cards of the same value.
  • Straight: Five consecutive cards (e.g., 2-3-4-5-6).
  • Flush: Five cards of the same suit.
  • Full House: One pair and a three of a kind.
  • Four of a Kind: Four cards of the same value.
  • Straight Flush: Five consecutive cards of the same suit.
  • Royal Flush: A 10, Jack, Queen, King, and Ace of the same suit.
Dealing of Cards Each player receives two face-down cards ("hole cards"). After this, the dealer distributes five community cards face-up in three stages:
- **The Flop:** The first three community cards.
- **The Turn:** The fourth community card.
- **The River:** The fifth and final community card.
Players combine their hole cards with the community cards to form the best hand.
Betting
  • Check: Pass the turn without betting.
  • Bet: Wager a certain number of chips.
  • Call: Match another player's bet.
  • Raise: Increase the bet.
  • Fold: Surrender the hand.
Three face-up cards are then dealt on the table (the "flop"). Two more face-up cards are dealt one at a time (the "turn" and the "river"). Players aim to form the best possible hand using their hole cards and the community cards on the table.
Winner At the end of the betting round, players reveal their cards. The player with the highest-ranking hand according to the above classification wins.
